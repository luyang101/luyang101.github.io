Information Resource
  • BLS scope: The Bureau of Labor Statistics reports by occupation, industry, and state, not by individual airline company.
  • Company-level data isn’t disclosed
  • Actual company pay is specified in union contracts (CBAs) as hourly rate tables by aircraft type and seniority (year-of-service)
  • Annualized earnings vary widely with aircraft, seniority (FO vs Captain), credit hours (guarantee 70–75 hrs/month, plus extra flying), override premiums, per diem, and deadhead/holiday rules.
Airline Pilot Salary Trends (2020–2024)
  • Data source: BLS OEWS (occupation & state level). Some states have incomplete data; only states with relatively full series are shown. North Carolina: Missing 2020 salary in BLS. Used 2021 as baseline.
  • Trends:
  • Strong nominal and real growth in Oregon (+105% nominal, +69% real) and Minnesota (+79% nominal, +48% real).
  • Arizona also shows solid gains (+52% nominal, +26% real).
  • North Carolina saw a decline (–11% nominal, –26% real).
  • Alaska, Colorado, Texas, and Utah had only small nominal gains, which were negative in real terms
